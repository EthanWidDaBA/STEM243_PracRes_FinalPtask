Peroxisomes are membrane-bound organelles found in all eukaryotic cells, playing crucial roles in various metabolic processes. They are particularly known for their involvement in lipid metabolism, including the breakdown of fatty acids, and the detoxification of harmful substances. A key function is their role in generating and neutralizing hydrogen peroxide (H2​O2​), a reactive and potentially toxic byproduct of their oxidative reactions, which they convert into water and oxygen using enzymes like catalase.
